# Brand Sponsorship & Unboxing: A Beginner's Guide

A practical starting point for creators doing lifestyle content — GRWM, unboxing, hauls — who want to work with brands.

---

## 1. How Brand Deals Actually Work

There are a few different arrangements, and they're not the same thing even though creators use the terms loosely:

| Type | What it means | Do you get paid? |
|---|---|---|
| **Gifting** | Brand sends free product, no obligation to post | No — payment is the product itself |
| **Affiliate** | You post a link/code, earn a % of resulting sales | Yes, but only on sales you drive |
| **Paid sponsorship** | Brand pays a flat fee for a post/video | Yes, negotiated upfront |
| **Ambassadorship** | Ongoing relationship, recurring content + payment or product | Yes, usually recurring |
| **Affiliate + gifting hybrid** | Free product now, commission on future sales | Both |

Most creators start at gifting/affiliate and move toward paid sponsorships as their numbers and negotiating leverage grow.

---

## 2. Getting Your First Gifted Products

Brands look for a few basic things before sending free product:

- **A consistent posting niche** — unboxing/GRWM/lifestyle content, posted regularly (not a one-off)
- **An engaged audience**, even if small — comments and saves matter more than raw follower count at this stage
- **A clean, professional bio** with contact info (business email, not just DMs)
- **Media kit** (see Section 4) — even a one-pager makes you look serious

**How to get noticed:**
- Tag brands genuinely in organic posts before ever pitching them — this puts you on their radar
- Join affiliate/creator programs directly (see Section 6) — many brands have an open signup
- Reach out cold with a short, specific pitch (Section 5)
- Use platforms that connect creators with brands: **ShopMy, LTK (LikeToKnowIt), Grin, Aspire (formerly AspireIQ), Creator.co**

---

## 3. Affiliate Marketing — Often More Lucrative Than Sponsorship

For unboxing/haul content specifically, affiliate income is frequently the bigger earner, since you're already showing products.

**Main affiliate programs to know:**
- **Amazon Influencer Program** — requires a qualifying social following, gives you a storefront + trackable links
- **LTK (LikeToKnowIt)** — popular for fashion/beauty/home, app-based shopping links
- **ShopMy** — increasingly popular, lower barrier to entry, works across brands
- **Direct brand affiliate programs** — many DTC (direct-to-consumer) brands run their own via ShareASale, Impact, or Refersion

**How commissions typically work:**
- Usually 3–20% of the sale, depending on category and program
- Paid on a delay (30–60 days is common, to account for returns)
- Tracked via unique links or discount codes — always use yours, never post without it

## 4. Building a Simple Media Kit

A media kit is a one-page pitch document brands use to evaluate you. Keep it to one page.

**Include:**
- Your name/brand name and a professional photo
- Follower counts and platforms (be honest — brands can verify)
- Engagement rate (likes+comments ÷ followers, roughly — tools like Later or Social Blade estimate this)
- Audience demographics if you have access (age range, top locations, gender split — found in platform analytics)
- 2–4 examples of past content (screenshots or links)
- Content niche/style in one sentence
- Contact email

**Tools to build one for free:** Canva has ready-made media kit templates.

---

## 5. Pitching a Brand Cold

Keep it short. Brands get flooded with generic pitches — specificity gets responses.

**Structure:**
1. One line on who you are and your niche
2. One line on why *this specific brand* (mention a real product of theirs)
3. What you're proposing (unboxing video, GRWM feature, etc.)
4. Link to your media kit or recent content
5. Clear ask (gifted product, paid collab, or open to discuss)

Avoid vague asks like "would love to collab!" — brands respond better to a concrete proposal they can say yes/no to quickly.

| Platform | Best for |
|---|---|

| **LTK** | Fashion, beauty, home — shoppable content |

| **#ad / brand tags on Instagram, TikTok** | Organic discovery — brands search these |

## 7. Money & Legal Basics

- **FTC disclosure is required** — any gifted, paid, or affiliate content must be disclosed (#ad, #sponsored, or platform's built-in "paid partnership" tag). This isn't optional; it's US federal law (FTC guidelines) and most other countries have equivalents.
- **Track everything** — gifted product value, affiliate income, sponsorship fees. Gifted product can count as taxable income above a certain value threshold depending on your country — worth a conversation with a tax preparer once this becomes regular.
- **Get agreements in writing** — even a simple email confirming deliverables, payment, and usage rights (can the brand repost your content? For how long?) protects both sides.
- **Negotiate usage rights separately from posting** — brands sometimes want to reuse your content in their own ads; that's a bigger ask than a single post and should come with more pay.

---

## 8. Realistic Progression

| Stage | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| **Start** | Organic tagging, signing up for open affiliate programs (Amazon, ShopMy) |
| **3–6 months in** | First gifted products from smaller/DTC brands, consistent posting |
| **6–12 months** | Media kit built, first paid micro-sponsorships, affiliate income becomes steady |
| **12+ months** | Recurring brand relationships, higher-value paid deals, possibly agency representation |

Growth isn't linear and depends heavily on niche and platform algorithm shifts — treat this as a general shape, not a guarantee.

## Quick-Start Checklist

- [ ] Pick your niche and post consistently for a few weeks before pitching anyone
- [ ] Sign up for Amazon Influencer Program (if eligible) and ShopMy
- [ ] Build a one-page media kit in Canva
- [ ] Start tagging brands organically in your posts
- [ ] Draft a short cold-pitch template
- [ ] Learn your platform's paid-partnership disclosure tool
- [ ] Set up a simple spreadsheet to track gifted items, affiliate income, and sponsorship fees
